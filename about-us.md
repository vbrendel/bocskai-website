        <span class="section-label">Who We Are</span>
        <h2>A School Built on Community &amp; Culture</h2>
        <p>
          The Bocskai Hungarian School is an accredited community language school providing
          Hungarian language and culture classes from baby playgroup to high school, taught
          exclusively by qualified native teachers.
        </p>
        <p>
          We operate as a non-profit entity, partially self-funded and supported by the
          Department of Education and Training and the Hungarian Reformed Church of Fitzroy North.
          Our school board provides strategic guidance to ensure we continue to meet the learning
          needs of our students.
        </p>
        <p>
          Classes are held every second Sunday between 9:00am and 12:30pm at the Bocskai
          Community Centre, 123 St Georges Road, Fitzroy North, Melbourne.
        </p>

<section class="section section-light">
  <div class="container">
    <div class="section-header">
      <span class="section-label">Our History</span>
      <h2>How We Grew</h2>
      <p>From a single class to a full community school — our journey since 2011.</p>
    </div>

    <div class="timeline">
      <div class="timeline-item">
        <div class="timeline-year">2011</div>
        <h3>School Founded</h3>
        <p>The Bocskai Hungarian School was established through cooperation with the Hungarian Reformed Church of Fitzroy North, beginning with a small group of dedicated families.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="timeline-item">
        <div class="timeline-year">2012</div>
        <h3>Kindergarten Established</h3>
        <p>A dedicated kindergarten program was added to serve our youngest learners, with age-appropriate activities in Hungarian language and culture.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="timeline-item">
        <div class="timeline-year">2013</div>
        <h3>Csemete Playgroup Launched</h3>
        <p>The Csemete baby-mum playgroup was introduced, creating a welcoming space for families with children aged 0–3 years to engage with Hungarian language and songs together.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="timeline-item">
        <div class="timeline-year">2014</div>
        <h3>School Library Created</h3>
        <p>Our library was established with a growing collection of Hungarian children's books, encouraging students to read at home between classes.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="timeline-item">
        <div class="timeline-year">2016</div>
        <h3>Department of Education Accreditation</h3>
        <p>The school received official accreditation from the Victorian Department of Education and Training, recognising our quality of teaching and governance.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="timeline-item">
        <div class="timeline-year">2017</div>
        <h3>Second Campus &amp; Confirmations</h3>
        <p>A second campus was opened to accommodate growing enrolments. The year also saw the first student confirmations held at the school, deepening our ties with the church community.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="timeline-item">
        <div class="timeline-year">2020</div>
        <h3>Online Learning During Pandemic</h3>
        <p>Like all schools, we transitioned to online education during COVID-19 lockdowns, demonstrating the resilience of our school community and teachers.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="timeline-item">
        <div class="timeline-year">Today</div>
        <h3>A Thriving Community School</h3>
        <p>We continue to grow, welcoming new families from across Melbourne and maintaining our commitment to high-quality Hungarian language and cultural education.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
  </div>
</section>

<section class="section" id="classes">
  <div class="container">
    <div class="section-header">
      <span class="section-label">Our Programs</span>
      <h2>Classes for Every Age</h2>
      <p>
        Each class is carefully designed for its age group, with curriculum tailored to
        develop language skills in a fun and culturally rich environment.
      </p>
    </div>

    <div class="grid-2">

      <div class="card">
        <div class="card-body">
          <span class="class-age">0–3 years</span>
          <h3>Csemete — Baby-Mum Playgroup</h3>
          <p>
            Our Csemete playgroup is a welcoming space for parents and their babies or toddlers to
            experience Hungarian language through songs, rhymes, finger games, and play. It's as much
            for the parents as the children!
          </p>
        </div>
      </div>

      <div class="card">
        <div class="card-body">
          <span class="class-age">Kindergarten</span>
          <h3>Mesemanók — Fairy-tale Folk</h3>
          <p>
            Nursery rhymes, Hungarian folk tales and storytelling form the basis of this class.
            Children develop listening skills and Hungarian vocabulary through imaginative play and crafts.
          </p>
        </div>
      </div>

      <div class="card">
        <div class="card-body">
          <span class="class-age">Prep</span>
          <h3>Napraforgók — Sunflowers</h3>
          <p>
            Prep-age children begin formal literacy in Hungarian: letter recognition, phonics, reading
            preparation and simple writing. Activities are playful and age-appropriate.
          </p>
        </div>
      </div>

      <div class="card">
        <div class="card-body">
          <span class="class-age">Grades 1–2</span>
          <h3>Levelezőtársak — Pen Pals</h3>
          <p>
            Students build reading and writing skills through pen-pal style activities, encouraging
            real-world communication in Hungarian. Grammar fundamentals are introduced gently.
          </p>
        </div>
      </div>

      <div class="card">
        <div class="card-body">
          <span class="class-age">Grades 3–4</span>
          <h3>Kincskeresők — Treasure Seekers</h3>
          <p>
            Hungarian myths, legends and folk tales provide the backdrop for developing vocabulary,
            reading comprehension and writing skills. Students "seek the treasure" of language.
          </p>
        </div>
      </div>

      <div class="card">
        <div class="card-body">
          <span class="class-age">Grades 5–6</span>
          <h3>Kalandorok — Adventurers</h3>
          <p>
            Idioms, proverbs, creative writing and spoken communication take centre stage.
            Students develop confidence in expressing themselves fluently in Hungarian.
          </p>
        </div>
      </div>

      <div class="card">
        <div class="card-body">
          <span class="class-age">Grades 7–10</span>
          <h3>Felfedezők — Explorers</h3>
          <p>
            Project-based learning and VCE Hungarian exam preparation. Students explore
            Hungarian culture, history and literature at a deeper level, building skills
            for formal assessment.
          </p>
        </div>
      </div>

      <div class="card">
        <div class="card-body">
          <span class="class-age">Adults</span>
          <h3>Adult Classes (Occasional)</h3>
          <p>
            We occasionally run adult Hungarian language classes for parents and community members
            who wish to improve their language skills. Check with us for current availability.
          </p>
        </div>
      </div>

    </div>
  </div>
</section>
